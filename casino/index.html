Explore vs. exploit.

Every round, one dilemma: cash in on the machine that looks best, or gamble a pull on another to learn more?

Exploit

Pull the best win-rate so far — grab reward now.
Risk: your "best so far" might just be early luck.

Explore

Try another machine to gather information — it might be better.
Cost: you spend a pull on a probably-worse option.

The ε-greedy strategy.

Each round:Explore with probability ε, otherwise Exploit.
So ε sets how often you gamble on learning.

Choosing Epsilon

0pure greedy — never explores
0.1the sweet spot — explore just enough
1pure random — never exploits

Check your intuition.

With ε = 0, how does the strategy behave?

ε is the probability of exploring. At ε = 0 the explore branch never fires, so the agent only exploits — and can stay locked on a machine that was merely lucky early.

Reading the regret curves.

Why does a large fixed ε (like 0.5) keep its regret climbing round after round?

Once you've found the best machine, every exploratory pull is wasted reward. A fixed ε = 0.3 explores 30% of the time forever, so its regret keeps climbing roughly linearly — the cost of over-exploration. (Going too small, like ε = 0.01, has the opposite danger: it may never explore enough to find the best machine at all.)
